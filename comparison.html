Tool-by-tool comparison

How Composure compares to what you're already using

These are excellent tools. They're just not built for MSP client communication during service events. That's not a criticism — it's simply a gap that Composure was built to fill.

PagerDuty & OpsGenie
Alert management and on-call tools — invaluable for engineers, not designed for clients
Excellent for alerting engineers, managing on-call rotas, and routing incidents to the right responders.
Not designed to draft professional, role-calibrated communications for client stakeholders.
Cannot generate post-incident reports or AM briefings.
Composure works alongside PagerDuty — connecting to it to receive incident data and then handling all client communication from that point.
Statuspage (Atlassian)
Public status pages — one message for all, no personalisation, no context
Excellent for publishing public-facing system status in a structured, branded format.
One generic message to all subscribers — no role-based tailoring, no per-contact personalisation.
No internal AM briefings, no post-incident reports, no AI-drafted communications.
Composure includes a client status page — and goes much further with private, personalised, role-calibrated communications for each stakeholder.
ConnectWise & Autotask (PSA Tools)
Ticket and workflow management — the backbone of your operations, not a communication platform
Essential for managing tickets, workflows, time entries, billing, and internal operations.
Ticket notifications are technical and transactional — not suitable for professional client communications during incidents.
No AI generation, no role-based tailoring, no stakeholder management, no post-incident reports.
Composure integrates directly with ConnectWise and Autotask to pull ticket data — so the AI always has the context it needs to draft accurate, timely communications.
ServiceNow & Freshservice (ITSM)
Enterprise ITSM platforms — powerful for large IT teams, not designed around MSP client communication
Powerful for enterprise ITSM workflows, change management, CMDB, and large internal IT departments.
Not designed around the MSP model — multiple clients, multiple stakeholders, external client communication as the primary requirement.
Significant cost, complexity, and implementation overhead for the MSP use case.
Composure is built specifically for the MSP model — multiple clients, multiple contacts per client, role-based communication, and the professional service environment MSPs operate in.
Generic email tools (Outlook, Mailchimp, HubSpot)
Marketing and general email platforms — require manual writing, have no incident context, and were never designed for this
Require someone to manually write every communication — under pressure, during an incident.
No connection to your PSA or ITSM data — every update requires manual context input.
One message to all recipients — no role-based tailoring or stakeholder personalisation.
No audit trail, approval workflow, or governance over what's being communicated to clients.
No post-incident reports, no AM briefings, no scheduled holding updates.
Designed for marketing and newsletters — not for professional service event communication.
Composure replaces the manual email — not by automating the same bad process, but by building a purpose-designed communication system that your MSP can rely on under pressure.
